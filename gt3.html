    Performence & price Media


    • Flaunting a huge rear wing, giant air intakes, and fender-filling wheels and tires, there's no mistaking the GT3 RS for anything but the track-ready racer it is. Luckily, neutral handling makes the RS less terrifying than it looks. The 500-hp 4.0-liter flat-six howls right up to its 8800-rpm redline, driving the rear wheels through a seven-speed dual-clutch automatic. The more discreet-and already sold out-911 R loses the rear wing and swaps the automatic for a sweet-shifting six-speed manual.
